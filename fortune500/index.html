The Rise and Fall of Asia's Enterprises

The Fortune Global 500, also known as Global 500, is an annual ranking of the top 500 corporations worldwide as measured by revenue. The list is compiled and published annually by Fortune magazine.

By Yuqing Liu

The Transitions of Asia-based Enterprises

Over two decades of Fortune 500 rankings, Japan has been significantly leading in the 90s and early 21st century. Until recent years, China starts to be a force to be reckoned with in the enterprise world.

The Trend of Chinese Companies

Of the Fortune Global 500 in 2021, 135 companies are from China, making up 60% of Asia's enterprises. Back in 1995, there were only 3 Chinese companies ranked in the list.

Most companies have a steep rise in their rankings, especially after 2006. State Grid from China is the top Asian company in 2021.

While the presence of Chinese firms in the Fortune Global 500 has been mostly state-owned in the past, there have been several technology corporations like Huawei, Alibaba that managed to climb the list.

Only a few companies didn't benefit from the boom.

The Lost Japan

Of the 158 Asia-based companies in 1995, 146 were from Japan. Nowadays, there are 52 companies, making up 23% of the enterprise market in Asia.

Many of them have been hit hardly by the Asian financial crisis around 1997.

Until 2021, Only 5 companies have been risen since they entered on the list. Among them, Japan Post Holdings joined in 2004 and experienced rapid growth since 2006.

South Korea's Turbulence

Over two decades, it has been normal that South Korean companies joined and left, many stay on the list shortly. The number of companies in the Fortune Global 500 list increased slowly, more companies were included from 8 in 1995 to 15 this year.

Seven companies saw their rankings go up from 1995, two of them are the leading ones. Samsung Electronics, Hyundai Motor are included in the top 100. POSCO, a steel-making company, joined the list on 2002 ranking 494th, it grew rapidly ranking as the 226th corporations by the Fortune Global 500 in 2021.

More than a dozen of companies saw their rankings fall down from 1995 to 2021. Most of them stayed on the list very shortly.

Latecomers - Southeast Asia

Fourteen Southeast Asian companies made the Fortune Global 500 in 2021. Thailand, Malaysia and Indonesia had one each and four are from Singapore, seven are from India. Most of them joined after 2004.

The rising companies started at inconsequential positions when they joined the list. They made the rapid growth shortly. Until 2021, two Indian enterprises, Reliance Industries and State Bank of India, are rising, same as Thailand's PTT and Singapore's Wilmar International.

The declining ones joined and left the list along the way. Most of them stayed less than ten years.

According to reports from Fortune.com, the Fortune Global 500 companies generated $31.7 trillion in revenue, accounting for more than a third of global GDP.

Aisa-based enterprises make up 45% of the list, raising their contribution from 160 in 1995 to 224 in 2021. The geographical company distribution of rankings has changed dramatically. The rise and fall has put the economic powers on a collision course.

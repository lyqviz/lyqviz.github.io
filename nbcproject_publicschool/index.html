Non-Computing Devices Access Disparity in the US Households

in school district level

Millions of school children live in households without access to any computing device (including tablets, smartphones, laptops and desktops). This graphic visualizes the disparity among households with school children while without any computing device in each school district across the country.

Each circle is a school district, circles in blue indicate households living in the school district where have higher portion of population without access to any computing device, circles in yellow are those with fewer portion of population without access to any computing device.

6 out of top 10 most computing-device-needed households are in school districts from Arizona. The rest are from New York, Indiana and Georgia.
